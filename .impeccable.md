## Design Context

### Users
Onyx users who use Craft to turn company knowledge, uploaded files, and connected apps into working artifacts such as web apps, documents, dashboards, and presentations. They are often in an active creation or review flow and need confidence that the agent is making progress without losing transcript clarity.

### Brand Personality
Calm, capable, work-focused. Craft should feel like a precise agentic workspace: transparent enough to build trust, restrained enough for repeated professional use, and responsive while long-running work is underway.

### Aesthetic Direction
Operational and refined, aligned with the existing Opal system and Craft's three-panel workspace. Agent progress should take cues from ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Lovable, and current agentic UX patterns: compact live status, progressive disclosure, visible activity, and quiet affordances instead of large decorative loading panels.

### Design Principles
- Keep the transcript scannable by default; expose deeper agent activity through compact, opt-in disclosure.
- Make long-running work visibly alive with small motion cues that do not compete with tools, answers, or approvals.
- Prefer existing Opal/Craft primitives, icons, spacing, and colors over new visual systems.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences and keep animations transform/opacity-based.
- Use state visibility to build trust: show that Craft is thinking, acting, or waiting without overexplaining the UI.
